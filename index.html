Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • Sugar
  • Maida (flour)
  • Oil or Ghee

Recipe

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, flour, baking soda, and cardamom powder.
  2. Add ghee and mix until the mixture is crumbly.
  3. Slowly add milk, little by little, to form a soft dough. Don't knead it too hard, just bring it together.
  4. Let the dough rest for about 10 minutes.
  5. In the meantime, prepare the sugar syrup: In a saucepan, combine water and sugar. Heat until the sugar dissolves completely, then add cardamom powder and rose water (optional). Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes and keep it warm.
  6. Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan over low to medium heat.
  7. Divide the dough into small portions and gently roll them into smooth balls. Be careful not to apply too much pressure while rolling.
  8. Once the oil is heated, gently drop the balls into the oil. Fry the gulab jamuns on low heat, stirring occasionally, until they are golden brown.
  9. Remove the fried gulab jamuns and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for 30 minutes.
  10. Serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your delicious Gulab Jamuns!

Rasmalai

Ingredients

  • 1 packet of rasgullas (or homemade rasgullas, 8-10 pieces)
  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder

Recipe

  1. Start by squeezing out the syrup from the rasgullas, if using store-bought ones. Gently press them to remove excess syrup and set aside.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the full-fat milk to a boil. Once it boils, reduce the heat to medium and let it simmer. Stir occasionally.
  3. Add the sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron (along with its milk) to the simmering milk. Stir well and let it cook for 10-15 minutes until the milk thickens slightly.
  4. Add the heavy cream to the milk mixture and continue simmering for another 5 minutes to get a rich consistency.
  5. Once the milk has thickened to your desired consistency, remove it from heat. You can also add rosewater or kewra water for a floral aroma, if desired.
  6. Now, gently immerse the rasgullas into the warm milk mixture. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes (you can refrigerate it for a longer period for better flavor).
  7. Garnish the rasmalai with chopped pistachios, almonds, and any remaining saffron strands.
  8. Serve chilled and enjoy!
